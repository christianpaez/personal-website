This is a go-to for anyone who has just got a new puppy. It’s short, concise and, on top of that, free.

### Good ol’ positive reinforcement approach

If you look online for opinions on Zak George’s content, a lot of people will probably tell you that there is nothing new under the sun and training dogs using the positive reinforcement method is not groundbreaking. They are probably right, but nonetheless, the author did a great job here creating this introductory material.

The ideas here, in my opinion, are good enough to raise a decent puppy. I have tried a lot of these plus stuff I found in other books with 2 of my dogs (Border collie female, Golden Retriever male) and they really work well; nothing is stopping you from trying different approaches, but what’s in this book is good enough and efficient.

### Bonding and fun

This is the main takeaway I got from the book. The point of having a dog, I believe, should be bonding with the most loyal creature on planet earth and having fun. Yes, the adaptation process will take time, tears and frustration, but it will give you a new best friend for many years to come. This book is for sure a great way to start this journey without spending a penny.

### Go or No-Go?

Yes, 100%. Go read it (if you can still find it).
