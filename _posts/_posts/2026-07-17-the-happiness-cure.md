I picked this one up from the local library, price tag wasn’t too crazy, the title was engaging and it seemed like a departure from the usual ‘20 things that X people do’ or ‘Habits of the super successful’ books I was used to seeing in the library, so I bought it.

## Science still dominates when it comes to wellness

I have read a good amount of wellness/self-help books and they usually fall into one of 2 categories, the scientific ones or the philosophical ones. This one goes deeper into the scientific category(even though there are great practical tips too) and that’s what makes it shine. There is just way too much science(Psychology, Medicine, Physiology, Biology) that does a great job explaining why we feel the way we do; wellness books do discuss that but not as deep as this one to be honest.

## Modern vs Old brain

Is it refreshing to understand that a lot of the things we do that are problematic, go way back to biology and how we lived hundreds of years ago. This explains why is not so easy to build a healthy lifestyle that thrives over time and why we fallback to bad habits from the past. The author here focuses on common problems we have as modern humans and gives advice on how to regulate our advanced monkey brain.   

## Human toxic instincts

We are intelligent as a species but we cannot ignore that we feel a lot, on every single aspect of our life, and this drives a huge amount on the way we operate daily. That is probably the best lesson from the book; we need to embrace that we are human nonetheless and our instincts haven’t evolved as much as our emotional brain. To regulate this, the author advice is in favor of exercising, you may think is a bit cliche, but he is not wrong; the facts and research he shows is spot on and from personal experience(and people close to me), I can confirm nothing makes you feel better than a steady exercise schedule.

## Should you read it?

Yes, luckily this book was a great choice, good price tag. I gave it away to one of my in-laws and she said it was great(she actually read it, good sign).
