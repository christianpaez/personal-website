I got this book a while ago from a secondhand library, basically for pennies. I paid like 2 bucks for it. Usually, second-handed books are a coin toss in terms of quality and content, but this book did not disappoint. I do not think this book is available in English, but I will write the review in English anyway.

## Nutritional Science

There is a lot to uncover when it comes to nutrition. A fitness coach told me years ago: ‘There is more we do not know about nutrition than what we actually know’. For this reason, books about nutrition tend to focus on singular aspects of its science and end up feeling biased. However, the author of this book did actually go fairly in depth when it comes to scientific studies and consensus to the point where I did feel like I was learning instead of being lectured.

## Our relationship with food

I do not think I have met 2 people in this world that had the same relationship with food. It always comes out as a very personal and subjective decision. Each person probably has different things in mind when they decide what they do or do not eat, how much, when, etc. General tips are given by the author, and she even suggests taking these with a grain of salt instead of taking them as mandatory rules, again, props to her for not lecturing the reader and keeping an unbiased stance.

## Nutrition myths

Social media have been flooded with nutritional hacks and the like, coming from your friends, neighbors, local personalities and even movie stars. Some of these ideas have become so popular that they become almost like general knowledge, even though we may not have evidence to back that up with certainty. Monica goes through a couple of those in this book, which is fantastic for a reader that is skeptical about things posted online. As an example, one of the myths mentioned that I almost never see online is that there isn't really ‘good’, nor ‘healthy’ or ‘bad’ food, it’s more like a tradeoff between calories, taste, micronutrients, price and some other factors. Some foods may make sense for you but not for other people.

## Go or Pass?

I think the book is great, condensed, not too long nor too short, varied and moreover, useful; if you can get your hands on it, go for it.
