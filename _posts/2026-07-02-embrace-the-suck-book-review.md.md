After the Navy SEAL mindset began trending on YouTube, with people like Jocko Willink and David Goggins, I saw this book online and decided to give it a go; stories about Navy SEALs sound great, and I wanted to at least learn what they do and if there is anything I can try.

## Comfort in Discomfort?

This was the main idea of the book, in my opinion. The author has various ways and scenarios describing different nuances of what I process as just being as comfortable as you can, even when you can’t. In fact, sometimes being in discomfort can be good. Usually, when we are outside our comfort zone, it is when we learn new things, since discomfort creates a challenge in our brain. You and I have probably heard something like that already, but I remember Eric Mead (an awesome close-up magician) saying in a conference that the shows that he enjoyed the most were the ones where something bad happened; because that’s when he had to go back, revisit his performance and improve it. Bill Malone (also an awesome close-up magician) also said at the same conference that his first gig doing magic in a restaurant/bar was a disaster. Every single day he messed up magic tricks, people were asking him to get another performer. However, that gig was when he learned what works and what is funny and also what isn’t.

## You got what you got, deal with it

This is another great point from this book. No matter what happens in your life, where you were born, where you live and other factors, complaining will not do anything, you can vent, but you are not moving forward nor doing anything, you are just paralyzed. This point is a bit harsh, but a nicer way to see it is to just be responsible for your life and try not to involve other factors in your situation.

## We are all going to die

Piggy-bagging on the previous point, one truth about your life, no matter who you are, is that you and I are going to die one day. We do not know when, nor how or why, so why not make this life as dynamic as possible? Take risks, do not complain, do not overthink, just keep moving. You are given a set of hands and 24 hours each day, make the best of them.

## So, would I recommend this book?

Totally, 100%. Some Navy SEAL stuff online seems cheesy, but the author did a great job here.

Also, the Magic Conference mentioned above where Eric Mead and Bill Malone spoke was the Essential Magic Conference from 2012 (14 years ago, clearly it left a mark).
